## About

Jop Hermans photographs one stretch of coastline. He has done so for years. The result is one of the most coherent bodies of coastal photography in the Netherlands.

His subject is the North Sea as it presents itself at Scheveningen — not as a tourist destination, but as a living, shifting environment that is never the same twice. Calm mornings where the water is glass and a single surfer breaks the horizon. Storm days when the pier disappears behind spray and the waves reach heights that make the city feel fragile. Sunsets so saturated they look artificial but are not.

What distinguishes Jop's work is restraint. Where other surf photographers reach for drama and action, he tends toward stillness. His best images often contain very little: a silhouette, a horizon line, the geometry of foam retreating over dark pebbles. The North Sea as a meditation rather than a spectacle.

He works in both colour and black-and-white, and the two bodies of work feel distinctly different — the colour work warm and atmospheric, the black-and-white work stark and graphic. Both are unmistakably his.

His work has been published in Carve Magazine (10 pages, 16 photographs), featured in 24 The Hague magazine, and installed as large-format prints in venues across Den Haag including Noordzee Boardstore. He sells fine art prints through his own Shopify store and through Rewide.

## On his photography

*"Mijn doel is eenvoudig: de pure schoonheid van de Noordzee delen met degenen die zich ermee verbonden voelen."*

He does not chase unusual conditions. He shows up consistently, in all weather, and pays attention. The extraordinary images come from that discipline — not from luck.
