## About

Léon van Bon spent twenty years chasing victories on a bicycle. Two Tour de France stage wins. National champion multiple times. Classics podiums, world championship results, a career that took him across every major race on the calendar.

When he retired in 2013, he picked up a camera.

The transition was less of a pivot than it might appear. Both disciplines demand the same thing: presence. The ability to be in the right place at the right moment, to read what is about to happen before it does, to act on instinct faster than thought.

As a rider, Léon knew every position on a peloton, every gesture that precedes an attack, every face a cyclist makes in the final kilometre. As a photographer, that knowledge became his lens.

He now covers all the major races — Tour de France, Giro d'Italia, Vuelta, the Spring Classics — with the access and understanding that only an ex-professional can have. Other photographers stand at the barriers. Léon understands which barrier to stand at, and why.

His work won Best Cycling Photo of 2024 at the Mark Gunter Photo Awards — the world's leading cycling photography competition — for an image of Tadej Pogačar crossing a Giro d'Italia finish while two skiers flew over the road above him. The kind of shot that requires both extraordinary timing and the instinct to know where to look.

> *"Als profwielrenner jaagde ik jarenlang op een mooie overwinning. Nu jag ik op mooie beelden. Met dezelfde passie en toewijding. Op zoek naar dat ene juweel."*

---

## On his photography

His eye is drawn to emotion over technique. He believes the most honest cycling photographs are made at the moments of maximum pressure — the final climb, the cobblestone section in the rain, the finish line. Where the effort is visible on every face and there is no energy left for performance.

He also finds beauty in the margins: the fan in an outrageous costume, the shadow of a bicycle on a road surface, the spray of a wheel on a wet descent. The things that happen around the race, not only in it.
