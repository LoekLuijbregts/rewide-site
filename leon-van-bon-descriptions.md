## 01 — Above the Giro
**Event:** Giro d'Italia 2024 — Alpine stage  
**Subject:** Tadej Pogačar in the maglia rosa, two freestyle skiers airborne above the finish line  
**Award:** Best Cycling Photo of 2024 — Mark Gunter Photo Awards  
**Tags:** Giro, Pogačar, cycling, alpine, skiing, surreal, sport, Italy

### Product page description
This photograph should not exist.

Two freestyle skiers are airborne above a Giro d'Italia finish line. Beneath them, Tadej Pogačar — in the maglia rosa, at the peak of one of the greatest seasons in professional cycling history — crosses the line alone.

The skiers were performing on a slope adjacent to the finish. The timing required to capture both in a single frame — the jump at its apex, the rider at the line — was not planned. It happened once. Léon was there.

This image won Best Cycling Photo of 2024 at the Mark Gunter Photo Awards, the world's leading cycling photography competition. The judges noted what any viewer immediately understands: it looks like AI. It isn't. It is simply what happens when someone who knows exactly where to stand is paying attention at the right moment.

*Winner, Mark Gunter Photo Awards 2024 — Professional Category*

## 02 — The Rainbow Jersey
**Event:** Spring Classics 2024 — Belgium  
**Subject:** World champion in full effort, crowd at the barriers  
**Tags:** classics, Belgium, world champion, cycling, sport, emotion, cobbles

### Product page description
The rainbow jersey does not hide.

Every rider who wears it becomes a target — for rivals, for fans, for cameras. Every climb, every cobbled sector, every moment of weakness is magnified. The pressure of the stripes is its own kind of suffering.

This image captures that pressure at its most visible. The face tells the story. There is nothing left to give, and everything left to find.

Léon van Bon made this photograph at the Spring Classics in Belgium — the races he competed in himself, on roads he knows by name. His access is not just physical. It is the understanding of what a rider looks like when they are truly at their limit, and how to be in the right position to show it.

*Fine art print. Particularly powerful in large format.*
