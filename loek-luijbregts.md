## About

Loek Luijbregts is not a photographer who cycles. He is a cyclist who photographs.

That distinction matters. His images come from places most photographers never reach — not because they are inaccessible, but because getting there requires days in the saddle, trust in the wrong direction, and a willingness to be the only person on a road that probably has no name on any map.

Over the past decade, Loek has cycled through more than 30 countries across Europe, Africa, the Middle East, and beyond. From the Pyrenees to the Ugandan Highlands, from the Cappadocian plateau to the gravel tracks of Cyprus. Each journey begins with a route and ends somewhere unexpected. His camera documents what happens in between.

His work is not about performance or adventure for its own sake. It is about the world that opens up when you slow down to the speed of a bicycle. The people who appear at the side of the road. The light that hits a landscape at an hour nobody else is awake to see. The details — a yellow van at the top of a pass, a flock of balloons at dawn, a single rider on a red dirt road — that contain entire stories if you know how to look.

Loek co-founded Cycling Incubators in 2020, during the first global lockdown, with the belief that cycling is one of the most honest forms of freedom available to a human being. His photography is the evidence.

## On his photography

*"I don't set up shots. I put myself in places where something might happen, and I wait. Sometimes I wait a long time. The best images are always the ones I didn't plan."*

*"Fietsers zoeken kracht en vrijheid. Ik zoek ontdekking en schoonheid. Die komen vaker samen dan je zou denken."*
